Professional background

I have always worked in communications in close contact with business partners from all over the world – so I am well used to the necessity of professionally attending the demands of others while being objective and empathetic.

In all my previous jobs I have developed content that was subsequently marketed to the outside world. As a freelancer, I have worked mostly doing translations – books, TV shows, movies, technical texts. I am fluent in English, Portuguese, Spanish and French, and have worked in several languages on a daily basis for most of my career.

Frequently Asked Questions (Really)

Would you like to know more about me? Chances are I was already asked these questions a dozen times, either in job interviews or in bars Surprisinly, the small-talk of both situations is very similar. Even Tinder dates start with the same ice-breaking questions.

So this is an honest FAQ, with real-life questions I have to answer more or less on a weekly basis.

  Why did you leave a career in publishing?

Working with publishing was a dream come true. I met nice people, went to Book Fairs, and got a position in the hottest publishing house in Brazil. But it didn't take long to realize that Publishing Business is
1) conservative;
2) averse to risks. In other words: the perfect place build a boring career.

  Why did you decide to become a web developer?

Five years ago, I decided I had enough. "So Long, and Thanks for All the Fish, Book World". With the abilities I had learned, I embarked on a two years journey working as a freelance translator and content writer. Before setting in Berlin, I have lived a couple of years as a digital nomad, working mostly with content editing and translation. But it wasn't until my recent experience as freelancer at Otto Radio that I realized that my set of skills are not enough to find a job in the digital marketplace. It was my first professional contact with digital marketing tools, and I soon realized that there is no room for gut feelings or educated guesses when dealing with actual data. I am therefore eager to get my hands dirty and learn more about SEO and content marketing.
